EU Data Processing Addendum (GDPR)

Document ownerLegal & Compliance — Data Protection
Version3.0
Effective date10 April 2026
Last reviewed10 April 2026
ClassificationControlled — Compliance
Applies toEU/EEA data subjects

This addendum sets out how Meridian Cloud processes personal data of EU/EEA data subjects in accordance with the GDPR. For EU personal data, the rules in this addendum override the defaults in the Global Data Retention Policy.

Right to erasure

On a valid erasure request from an EU data subject, or upon account closure, Meridian Cloud will delete the data subject's personal data within 30 days, subject to limited legal exceptions.

Important scope distinction

  • This 30-day erasure rule applies to personal data of EU data subjects.
  • It does not require deletion of financial transaction records that Meridian Cloud must retain to meet independent legal, tax, and audit obligations. Such records are retained per the Global Data Retention Policy, minimized and segregated from personal data where feasible.

In short: EU personal data is erased within 30 days of request or account closure; legally mandated transaction records are retained separately for 7 years. These two rules govern different data categories and are not in conflict.

Global Data Retention Policy

Document ownerLegal & Compliance
Version4.2
Effective date01 March 2026
Last reviewed01 March 2026
ClassificationControlled — Compliance
Applies toAll customer data, globally

This policy sets default retention periods for categories of data held by Meridian Cloud. Jurisdiction-specific addenda may impose stricter requirements for particular data categories or regions.

Retention by data category

Data categoryDefault retention periodBasis
Financial transaction records7 yearsTax and audit obligations
Contracts and legal records7 years after terminationLegal
System and security logs13 monthsSecurity operations
Customer personal dataDuration of account + default period belowBusiness need

Default retention for customer personal data is the life of the account plus 90 days, unless a stricter jurisdictional requirement applies. See the EU Data Processing Addendum for EU-specific personal-data rules, which override this default for EU data subjects.

Scope note

Retention periods apply by data category. Financial transaction records are retained for 7 years for tax and audit reasons even where the related customer has closed their account; this obligation is distinct from personal-data erasure rights.
